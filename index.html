Official Statement — Draft (United States Government )

Title: Emergency Measures to Protect Public Safety and Combat Sexual Exploitation and Human Trafficking

Purpose:
To set forth urgent authorities and procedures for safeguarding the public from severe threats — including threats to life, mental health, and personal dignity — arising from organized criminal activity such as child sexual exploitation, human trafficking, and related transnational networks. These measures provide the Government with temporary control over financial assets of suspected perpetrators while criminal and administrative proceedings are pending, and establish penalties and restrictions intended to disrupt and dismantle the networks that facilitate these crimes.

Scope:
These provisions apply to any individual, group, or organization reasonably suspected of involvement in criminal activity that poses a material threat to public safety, life, mental health, or reputation. They also apply where there is credible evidence of participation in the production, distribution, sale, or financing of sexual exploitation or human trafficking activities, including on the open internet and the dark web.

Authority and Emergency Financial Control:
Where a person or entity is investigated or formally declared suspicious under duly authorized Government or judicial procedures, the Government shall have authority to temporarily assume control over that person’s or entity’s financial assets and accounts for the duration of legal and administrative actions. During this period, affected individuals shall be prohibited from using, transferring, or accessing their bank accounts, investment accounts, or other financial assets without express Government authorization. Violations of this restriction shall be subject to criminal and civil penalties.

Limits on Personal and Investment Holdings for Suspected Parties:
Suspected individuals or entities may retain a de minimis amount not to exceed USD 50,000 in personal bank accounts for limited living expenses as determined by implementing regulations. Holding or maintaining investment or money-market accounts while under suspicion or investigation is expressly prohibited. Any undisclosed or covert financial accounts discovered in violation of these provisions shall be seized and the funds transferred immediately to a Government-administered Child Protection and Victim Assistance Account for use in victim recovery, care, and prosecutions.

Criminal Penalties:
	•	Any person found guilty of intentionally violating the financial-control provisions, or of continuing to operate or conceal financial accounts in furtherance of sexual exploitation or trafficking, shall be subject to imprisonment of up to 14 years and a monetary fine of up to USD 250,000.
	•	If a family member, associate, or community group is complicit in or aids such activity, they shall be subject to imprisonment of up to 10 years and a fine of up to USD 100,000, consistent with due process and applicable criminal statutes.

International Cooperation:
Recognizing the transnational nature of trafficking and online sexual exploitation, the United States shall work with international partners — including but not limited to India, Japan, Ukraine, and other cooperating nations — to strengthen enforcement, intelligence-sharing, asset-recovery, and victim-protection mechanisms. Multinational cooperation will support cross-border seizures, extradition where appropriate, and prevention of illicit financial flows that sustain these criminal enterprises.

Statistical Context (2017–2024):
Government and agency reviews covering the period 2017–2024 indicate significant scale and harm associated with sexual exploitation and trafficking networks. Reported figures during this period include:
	•	Approximately 234,000 girls subjected to body-part trafficking following fatal assaults; and
	•	Approximately 345,000 girls forcibly sold across borders and forced into sexual exploitation and pornography production, with content circulated or sold on both dark-web and open platforms.

(These figures are cited for policy context and will be further substantiated and updated by the appropriate investigative and statistical agencies.)

Implementation and Oversight:
Implementing regulations will define procedures for (1) designation of persons or entities as “suspected” or “subject to emergency financial control,” (2) emergency freezes and transfers of assets, (3) permitted minimal access to funds for basic living needs, (4) standards of evidence and judicial review to protect civil liberties, and (5) auditing and oversight mechanisms to ensure lawful, proportionate, and transparent use of authority. Safeguards will be provided to protect innocent parties and ensure timely restitution and remedy for victims and erroneously targeted persons.

Conclusion:
These emergency measures are intended to provide robust tools to protect public safety, disrupt criminal systems that traffic and exploit children, and ensure that illicit financial gains are seized and redirected to support victims and prosecutions. All actions under these provisions shall be carried out in accordance with the Constitution, federal law, and internationally recognized human-rights obligations.
